# Huckle — Cut From Culture

**URL:** https://wearehuckle.com/
**Locations:** London (Holborn + Shoreditch)
**Why this reference:** community-anchored independent shop, owner-led, real personality without forced quirk. Closest "owner is the brand" anchor for Z.

## Hero pattern

Full-bleed cinematic photograph of the actual storefront at night — warm shop interior glowing through the window, customers and barbers visible inside, blurred passer-by in motion on the sidewalk. White text card overlays bottom-left with a two-line tagline:

> Where Great Hair / Meets Real Culture & Community

No stock photography. The shop itself is the hero image.

## CTA

Single bright **orange/red "Book Now"** button below the hero, repeated in the header nav. Pushes to Fresha (third-party scheduling). The CTA is loud against the otherwise restrained palette — the only saturated color on the page.

## Color palette

- Black (logo, body text)
- White (text cards, backgrounds)
- Orange/red accent (CTA only — `#E04A2E` ish)
- Warm cream/wood tones (from photography)
- Photographic black + warm gold from the storefront-at-night shot

Restrained. The brand color is the photography itself.

## Typography

Lowercase wordmark logo with custom letterforms — slightly hand-drawn / informal feel. Body type is clean sans, geometric, plenty of breathing room. Headlines in slab/condensed character with monospace-feel.

## Photography style

Documentary. Real shop, real people mid-service, low light, warm tones. Looks shot at night. No models, no posed staff photos. The shop is the photo set.

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Hero as documentary storefront photograph** — Z's actual shop at golden hour with the barber pole + neighborhood context would land the same way.
- **CTA as the only saturated color** — entire palette restrained except the book button. Disciplined and effective.
- **Tagline structured as two-line declarative** — "Where ___ Meets ___" is borrowable; the slot can fill with whatever Z's positioning is ("Where Cuts Meet Westchester" / "Old-School Barbering for the Whole Family" / etc.).
- **Owner-led narrative section** — Huckle leans into Chris Ward's history. Z's bio (years cutting, why he opened in Westchester, why the hot-towel shave) deserves the same prominence.

## Not borrowable

- The London accent / record store angle is specific. Don't transplant it.
- Multi-location framing — Z is one shop.

## Mobile

Hero remains photographic and dominant. Tagline cards remain readable. CTA stays loud. No mobile-specific compromise.
