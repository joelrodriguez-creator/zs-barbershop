# House of Heritage — "Desert Dapper Cuts"

**URL:** https://houseofheritagelv.com/
**Location:** Las Vegas, NV
**Why this reference:** the heritage / hot-towel-shave / vintage-barbering peak of the spectrum. Sets a maximum on the "old-school done seriously" axis for Z's direction comparison.

## Hero pattern

Full-bleed dim cinematic photograph of the actual shop interior — barber chairs in symmetric rows, herringbone wood floor, framed B&W portraits on dark walls, vintage fixtures glowing warmly, mounted deer trophy visible. Centered title overlay:

> ACHIEVE LOOK OF DISTINCTION AT OUR ESTEEMED LAS VEGAS MEN'S GROOMING LOUNGE
>
> **WELCOME TO THE HOUSE OF HERITAGE**

The smaller line is supporting copy, the bigger line is the headline. Below: a single warm-mustard pill button:

> CHOOSE YOUR STYLIST

Note — not "book now," not "schedule." Choose your stylist. Frames the barber as the artisan you're selecting, not the shop as the vendor you're booking.

## CTA

Warm mustard / brass color pill (`#C8A35A`-ish) for the primary CTA. Outlined warm mustard "BOOK STYLIST" repeats in the header nav. The warm accent matches the brass / wood / vintage tones in the hero photography — color extracted from the actual shop.

## Color palette

- Near-black (background, text overlays)
- Warm dark brown / leather (from photography)
- Brass / mustard / warm gold accent (CTAs, wordmark accents)
- Cream / aged white (negative space, secondary type)
- Wood/herringbone warmth (from floor)

Pulled directly from the photographed interior. The brand color and the shop color are the same color.

## Typography

Display: bold condensed industrial sans, all caps, oversized. Reads "carved on a sign" — vintage Americana shop signage. Wordmark logo is illustrated with serif lettering inside an arch frame — heritage seal aesthetic.

Body type is a serif (legible classical face, possibly EB Garamond or Source Serif).

## Photography style

The single most striking move. The hero IS the shop, lit cinematically — deep shadows, warm pools of light, herringbone wood drawing the eye toward the back vanishing point. This is what a professional photographer with lighting equipment produces. Custom shot, definitely not stock, definitely not phone-snap.

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Cinematic photograph of the actual shop interior as the hero.** If Z's photo session can land one shot that does this — Z's actual chairs, his actual mirrors, golden-hour or evening-warm-bulb lighting — the entire design rests on it.
- **"Choose your stylist" framing** could translate to "Meet the barber" or "Sit with Z" — positioning the barber as the artisan, not the shop as the vendor. For a single-barber shop this lands even harder.
- **Brand color extracted from the actual shop interior.** If Z's shop has warm wood + brass + dark walls, the palette is already there. If it's bright fluorescent + green walls, the brand color is different — match it honestly.
- **Vintage Americana display type** for headlines while keeping body type readable serif. Heritage register without going full saloon-poster.
- **Heritage seal logo treatment** — Z's monogram inside an arched frame with "EST. 20XX" detail is a clean direction if Z wants old-school visual identity.

## Not borrowable

- "Look of Distinction" / "Esteemed Lounge" copy — too upscale for Z's $35 mid-market pricing. Adapt the visual register, drop the luxury copy.
- The "stylist" word — read as women's-salon-coded in a barber context. Use "barber."
- The Vegas-specific positioning. Z is Miami, not Vegas.

## Mobile

Hero photograph remains visible but the centered overlay text stacks tall — "WELCOME / TO THE / HOUSE OF / HERITAGE" wraps to 4 lines, which is dramatic and a bit overwrought. Tone down for mobile — let the photography breathe with less text overlay.
