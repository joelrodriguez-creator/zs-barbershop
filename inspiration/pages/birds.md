# Birds Barbershop — "Haircuts for all y'all"

**URL:** https://birdsbarbershop.com/
**Locations:** 9 in Austin, TX
**Why this reference:** strong personality voice without trying too hard. Friendly, inclusive, regional. The tagline is the brand.

## Hero pattern

Full-bleed editorial portrait of a real customer (different per page load — Birds rotates several). Bold italic display headline overlays bottom-left:

> HAIRCUTS FOR ALL Y'ALL

Two CTAs stacked below the headline: white outlined "APPLY" (recruiting) + bright lime-yellow filled "BOOK NOW". The lime is striking against the editorial photo and dark text.

Above the hero, the header sits on a pale cream strip with a custom illustrated logo (storefront silhouette with "BIRDS BARBERSHOP" in stacked sans-serif).

## CTA

**Bright lime yellow** (~`#D9F25E`) filled pill — book now. White outlined pill — apply. Both are oversized for confidence. The lime is the brand color and earns its loudness by being almost the only saturated thing on the page.

## Color palette

- Lime yellow (book CTA — the brand color)
- Cream / pale off-white (header strip, backgrounds)
- Black (logo, headlines, body)
- Coral pink (secondary cards lower on the page)
- Photographic editorial tones (warm skin tones, blue shirts, soft natural light)

## Typography

Bold italic condensed display sans for headlines — looks custom or heavily customized. Sans body. Logo is hand-illustrated. The italic display is what makes the brand feel confident and unbuttoned.

## Photography style

Real customers, plain studio backdrops, natural lighting, deadpan or playful expressions. Not posed beauty shots. The portrait above (a regular guy in a button-up making a fish-face) does more for "personality" than any tagline could. Photography is curated to feel inclusive — different ages, ethnicities, hair textures, genders.

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Bold italic display headline** with regional voice. Z's equivalent: "CUTS FOR THE WHOLE FAMILY" or "WESTCHESTER'S GOT YOUR FADE" or some Miami-coded phrase. Italic adds momentum without going aggressive.
- **One bright accent color** owns the entire brand identity. For Z that could be a warm mustard, a deep teal, a barbershop-pole red, or a vibrant Miami coral.
- **Real-customer portraits** beat polished marketing photography for "we're a real place, real people come here."
- **Booking popup pattern** — Birds' book button opens an in-page modal rather than redirecting to Square/Booksy. Z's no-online-booking site could do the same with the call/text CTA (modal with big phone + SMS buttons).
- **Free-beverage + mixtape personality details** mentioned in copy. Z's hot-towel-shave specialty + free coffee/cafecito for waiting customers = same energy.
- **9-location maturity treated as Austin pride, not chain identity.** Z is one shop — but the same energy works at one location.

## Not borrowable

- The Austin specificity. "Y'all" doesn't translate to Miami. Find the Miami / Westchester equivalent (Spanish lean? local slang? "vecinos"?).

## Mobile

Hero portrait crops cleanly to vertical. Lime CTA dominates. Apply CTA stacks above. Headline shrinks but stays bold.
