# Fresh Barbershop — "Your Friendly Neighborhood Barbershop"

**URL:** https://fresh-barbershop.com/
**Location:** Miami Lakes, FL
**Why this reference:** **DIRECT LOCAL COMPETITOR.** Family-owned (Edward + Aly Galdamez), kid-friendly (vending + arcade machines for waiting), Hispanic-owned, same general region as Z's. This is the most relevant comp in the lineup — and ironically the weakest design.

## Hero pattern

Background video (or large still) of a green plastic shop sign / scissors decoration. Confused composition — the visual is graphic-design clutter rather than a clear hero image. Header has minimal logo + nav. Three info columns float over the lower-third: Address (`16919 NW 67TH AVE / MIAMI LAKES, FL 33015`), Call Us (`(305) 909-9440 / FOR BOOKING`), Hours (`MONDAY THROUGH SATURDAY 9AM - 8PM SUNDAY 10AM - 4PM`).

The three-column trust strip at the bottom of the hero is the BEST move on the page. Address, phone, hours, immediately visible without scrolling — exactly what a local mobile visitor needs.

## CTA

The phone number IS the CTA. No "Book Now" button. The label says "FOR BOOKING" under the phone — phone-only positioning made explicit.

## Color palette

- Bright green (`#3DD96E`-ish) — the brand color
- Black (header, type)
- White (text on dark)
- Photographic interior (green/black/white shop)

The shop's actual interior is green-and-black, so the brand color matches reality. Honest. Strong.

## Typography

Generic sans-serif from a WordPress theme. Nothing distinctive. The condensed all-caps treatment of address/phone/hours works because it gets out of the way.

## Photography style

**Team photo on mobile.** The team — Eddie, Aly, the barbers — appears in matching Fresh t-shirts on the actual shop floor. Real people, real shop, no stock. Even the kids (Eddie's children, looks like) are in the photo. This is the page's other strong move.

Polaroid-frame styling on the team photo is twee but works — feels like a snapshot, not a brand shoot.

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Three-column trust strip at the bottom of the hero** (Address / Phone / Hours) is the single best UX move on any of the six reference sites. Z's address, (786) 281-8181, and Mon-Sat 9-7:30 should land in the same place — above the fold, no scrolling, big text. This pattern goes into the design no matter what direction wins.
- **Phone number as the primary CTA.** Matches Z's "phone + walk-in only" reality. The "FOR BOOKING" caption is the right framing.
- **Real team photo on mobile** with everyone in matching shop apparel = trust signal that no AI imagery can replicate. Schedule the photo session.
- **Honest match between brand color and actual shop interior.** If Z's shop has a distinctive color, lean into it. If not, pick a palette that matches the warmth/light of his actual space.
- **Family-owned framing** — Fresh wears it explicitly. Z's "good for kids" highlight + 5-star reputation built on word-of-mouth = same framing applies.

## Not borrowable

- The cluttered hero composition. The scissors-and-green-sign visual is busy without resolving into a focal point.
- The generic WordPress sans-serif. Z's site deserves real type direction.
- The polaroid-frame mobile treatment. Cute but dated.

## Mobile

Mobile drops the muddled hero and goes straight to the team photo + address/phone/hours block. Honest about the strengths — leads with people, gets to the contact info fast. Mobile is actually better than desktop here.
