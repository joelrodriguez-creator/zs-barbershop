# Fruits Hair Lab — "Fresh hair, calm space."

**URL:** https://fruitshairlab.com/
**Location:** Nashville
**Why this reference:** widely cited as a "personality-led" hair business website. **Important caveat:** Fruits is a women-leaning hair salon, NOT a barbershop. Borrow design moves, not positioning.

## Hero pattern

Two-column above-the-fold. Left: a "NASHVILLE HAIR STUDIO" pill chip + large serif headline ("Fresh hair, / calm space.") + supporting line + dual CTAs ("Schedule Consultation" filled dark + "View Services" outlined). Right: a stylized illustrated character (line-art face, peach + cream + sage accents) with an overlaid "Personal color, polished finish." card.

The illustration is the personality move — it's a custom character, not a stock photo, not a real headshot. It signals "this is a creative space, not a transactional one."

## CTA

Soft pill-shaped buttons in pure black with white text. Notably restrained for a "personality" site — the playfulness lives in typography and illustration, not in buttons.

## Color palette

- Soft peach (background gradient)
- Cream / off-white
- Warm rust accent for chip text
- Sage green (small accent in illustration)
- Near-black (typography, buttons)

A warm-pastel palette unusual for grooming sites. Most barbershops go dark. Fruits goes warm-light.

## Typography

Large serif headlines (Bodoni-like, high-contrast, slightly editorial). Sans body. Wordmark uses both — "Fruits" in serif, "Hair Lab" in serif italic with the rust accent.

## Photography style

**None used.** Illustration replaces photography in the hero. This is the most distinctive choice — the site's personality is carried entirely by typography, color, and one custom character illustration.

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Custom illustration as personality vehicle.** A line-art Z character (with quirky details — distinctive hair, glasses, signature pose) could carry "fun and quirky" without forcing it through copy. Z's actual visual identity could be a hand-drawn mark, not a photo.
- **Soft/warm palette in a male grooming context** is unusual — could be a way to land "quirky and friendly" without going edgy/dark.
- **Two-line declarative tagline with poetic compression** — "Fresh hair, calm space" is just 4 words. Compare to typical barbershop headlines that overpack.
- **Pill chip above headline** for context ("WESTCHESTER BARBERSHOP" / "MIAMI OLD-SCHOOL") — small but effective category framing.

## Not borrowable

- Female-leaning palette + illustration style if Z's audience expects masculine-coded barbershop signals.
- The salon positioning ("color", "consultation").
