# THE HOFS — "House of Fades"

**URL:** https://thehofs.net/
**Location:** Miami Lakes, FL
**Why this reference:** Miami-local + family-owned + hot-towel-finish specialty (matches Z's old-school shave specialty). Another direct comp, with a stronger design sensibility than Fresh.

## Hero pattern

Stark all-black background. Centered on the page: a beard-and-bowler-hat logo monogram in white with "HM / House of Fades" arched text. Below it, a sand-cream colored card with thin serif headline:

> Expert Haircuts and Grooming Services

Supporting line + black "BOOK NOW" pill button. Above the logo, the phone number `(305) 825-1824` sits on a dark sticky bar, always visible.

Below the hero card: a serif line in cream-on-black declares "Appointments are our priority. Walk ins are always welcome" — dual-mode positioning made explicit, an exact echo of Z's reality.

## CTA

Black "BOOK NOW" pill links to Schedulicity. Phone number CTA in the top bar is the secondary action. Walk-in friendliness is communicated in copy below the hero, not as a button.

## Color palette

- Pure black (~95% of the page)
- Soft sand / cream (`#D8D2C4`-ish) — used for typographic cards
- White (logo, type)
- Subtle warm cream from logo accents

The most disciplined dark palette in the lineup. Cream cards as the only contrast move are sophisticated — feels gallery-like, not "barbershop dark."

## Typography

Thin serif (Cinzel or similar — broad spacing, geometric, classical feel) for the headline. Slightly mismatched with the playful "HM / House of Fades" logo wordmark below. Body is sans, fine.

The serif choice is what differentiates this from generic dark-mode barber sites — most use blackletter or industrial sans. The Cinzel-like serif reads heritage without going Gothic.

## Photography style

**None visible above the fold.** The site relies entirely on typography against black. This is a significant choice — it either feels confident (gallery-like, minimal) or absent (where are the cuts, where's the shop?).

## Borrowable for Z's

- **Dark palette with cream typographic cards** as the only contrast — defensible if Z's photography isn't ready yet. Lets the site ship without leaning on imagery that doesn't exist.
- **Sticky phone bar at the top** with the phone number always visible. For a phone-booking shop this is the most important UX element. Always-on `tel:` link.
- **"Appointments... Walk ins... always welcome" dual framing** — exact match for Z's reality.
- **Thin classical serif for heritage signal** without going Gothic/blackletter. Cinzel, Cormorant, or Cardo would land similar.
- **Family-owned positioning + "complimentary drinks"** — Z's family-friendly + hot-towel-shave specialty are the same kind of credibility-anchoring detail.

## Not borrowable

- The total absence of imagery. Z's site should have photography (real eventually, AI placeholder until then). Black-with-typography-only is too austere for a "fun and quirky" shop.
- The Schedulicity dependency. Z is phone/walk-in only.
- The logo/headline typographic mismatch. Decide on one type voice.

## Mobile

Sticky phone bar moves to the top of the viewport — phone number becomes the first thing you see. Centered logo + headline card + book button stacks vertically. The minimal palette holds up on mobile better than busier sites do.
