# Z's Barbershop — Product

## Register

**brand** — design IS the product. This site is being delivered to Z as a polished "finished gift." The design itself communicates investment, taste, and the value Joel places on Z's friendship and craft. Quality of the site reflects on the quality of the cut. Joel's name doesn't appear on the public site — this is Z's site.

## Audience

**Primary:** Local prospective customers searching for a barbershop on a phone. They want to know — in this order — _can I trust this guy with my hair, is he nearby, what does it cost, how do I book._

**Secondary:** Existing customers who want to confirm hours, share the shop with a friend, or refer their kid.

**Tertiary:** Other barbers / industry people checking out Z's brand presence.

## Core pillars (reinforced on every page)

1. **Z is the craftsman.** Owner-operated, hands-on, gives a damn. The site should feel personal — not a chain, not a Webflow template, not a generic "haircuts near you."

2. **Classic and clean.** Z's craft is grounded in traditional barbering — cuts that look sharp, beards that look intentional, no gimmicks. The visual register should reflect that: typography with care, photography that feels lived-in, no AI-coded slickness.

3. **Easy to book and find.** Phone number prominent. Address prominent. Hours prominent. Mobile-first because that's where the audience is. Call-or-text is the default action on every page.

## Tone

- **Conversational, not corporate.** "Walk in or give us a ring" beats "Schedule your appointment today."
- **Confident, not boastful.** Show the work, don't sell the work.
- **Local.** Specific street, specific neighborhood, specific hours. Real place, real person.

## Honesty rules

- If services aren't offered, don't list them.
- If walk-ins aren't taken on a given day, say so plainly.
- Prices on the price list match the prices charged in-shop. If one moves, the other moves.

## What this site is NOT

- Not a booking system. Z books on his phone. The site facilitates contact, not transactions.
- Not a retail catalog. Z sells cuts, not pomades (yet).
- Not a blog. Z is a barber, not a content marketer.
- Not multi-location. One shop, one barber, one face.
