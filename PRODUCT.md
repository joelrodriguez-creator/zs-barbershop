## Register

## Audience

**Primary:** Local Westchester/West Miami prospective customers searching "barbershop near me" on a phone. They want to know — in this order — _can I trust this guy with my hair, is he nearby, what does it cost, how do I book._

**Secondary:** Existing customers (the 169-review base) who want to confirm hours, share the shop with a friend, or refer their kid.

**Tertiary:** Other barbers / industry people checking out Z's brand presence (Instagram → website conversion path).

**Implicit:** Hispanic / Latino West Miami residents — Westchester is heavily Cuban-American. Spanish-friendly tone considerations TBD with Joel (bilingual site? Spanish phrases sprinkled in? all-English?).

## Core pillars (reinforced on every page)

1. **Ziad is the craftsman.** Owner-operated, hands-on, 5-star reputation built on word of mouth and 169 actual Google reviews. The site should feel personal — not a chain, not a Webflow template, not a generic "haircuts near you." Z's personality (fun, quirky per Joel) leads the visual register.

2. **Old-school done right.** Z's specialty is the traditional hot-towel shave + classic cuts. Heritage-coded, not nostalgic-coded. Typography with care. Photography that feels lived-in and lit warmly, not airbrushed.

3. **Easy to book and find.** Phone number prominent. Address prominent. Hours prominent. Mobile-first because that's where the audience is. **"Call or Text to Book" is the default action on every page** — Z books on his phone, so the CTA matches reality.

4. **Family-friendly.** Z's profile highlights "good for kids." Surface this — a Dad searching "barber for my son" should see immediately that this shop welcomes kids. Likely a service-list line item (Kids' Cut) plus an implicit-welcome tone everywhere else.

## Tone

- **Conversational, not corporate.** "Walk in or give us a ring" beats "Schedule your appointment today."
- **Confident, not boastful.** Show the work, don't sell the work.
- **Local.** Specific street, specific neighborhood, specific hours. Real place, real person.

## Honesty rules

- If services aren't offered, don't list them.
- If walk-ins aren't taken on a given day, say so plainly.
- Prices on the price list match the prices charged in-shop. If one moves, the other moves.

## What this site is NOT

- Not a booking system. Z books on his phone. The site facilitates contact, not transactions.
- Not a retail catalog. Z sells cuts, not pomades (yet).
- Not a blog. Z is a barber, not a content marketer.
- Not multi-location. One shop, one barber, one face.
